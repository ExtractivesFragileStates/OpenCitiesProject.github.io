Open Cities is a joint project among the Global Facility for Disaster Reduction and Recovery (GFDRR) -- a partnership of 41 countries and 8 international organizations --the World Bank, and other organizations, to foster resilient communities in South East Asia. Open, accessible, and timely data is an integral tool for policymakers, disaster-assistance providers, and citizens alike -- ensuring informed preventive disaster risk mitigation efforts and assistance to disaster-affected areas.The launch of Open Cities includes case studies in three cities:

  • Dhaka, Bangladesh
  • Colombo, Sri Lanka
  • Kathmandu, Nepal

City-specific case studies -- including the opening up of previously closed datasets and the addition of new roads and disaster-specific points of interest for cities to OpenStreetMap -- represent innovative uses of open data to empower resilient communities.
